### Use Case 3: Outbound Sales Call Scoring

| Capability | Click2Dash | Olelo AI | Notes |
|------------|-----------|----------|-------|
| Outbound call tracking | Not explicitly mentioned | Not explicitly mentioned | **Critical gap for both** |
| Script compliance scoring | Not confirmed for outbound | Not confirmed for outbound | May only apply to inbound |
| Objection handling metrics | Not confirmed | Not confirmed | |
| Financing offer tracking | Not confirmed | Not confirmed | |
| Coaching/feedback | Yes (InsightsCast audio) | Yes (data-backed, personalized) | Different delivery methods |
| Real-time intervention | Not mentioned | Yes ("manager rescue") | Olelo advantage |

**Coverage Assessment**: Neither vendor explicitly addresses outbound sales call scoring. Both appear primarily focused on inbound call intelligence. This is the biggest gap in both solutions.

**Commentary**: This is a significant concern. Outbound sales — following up on estimates, calling back on declined services, upselling maintenance packages — is where revenue is directly generated. If neither vendor tracks outbound calls, you have two options:

1. **Ask both vendors directly**: "Can you score our outbound sales calls the same way you score inbound?" Get a definitive answer.
2. **Stack a dedicated tool**: Consider adding Rilla (field sales scoring) or a similar outbound-specific tool on top of whichever vendor you choose. This would increase total cost but close the gap.
3. **Custom solution**: This is exactly what our Call Intelligence Platform is designed to handle — both inbound and outbound scoring against custom playbooks.

The "manager rescue" feature from Olelo is the closest thing to outbound support — it alerts managers during live calls when intervention could save a deal. But this is reactive (during the call) rather than analytical (scoring after the call).

### Use Case 4: AI Phone Agent / After-Hours Coverage

| Capability | Click2Dash | Olelo AI | Notes |
|------------|-----------|----------|-------|
| 24/7 AI answering | Yes | Yes | Core feature for both |
| Appointment booking | Yes (automatic) | Yes (automatic) | Both handle scheduling |
| SMS alerts to customers | Not confirmed | Yes | Olelo confirms this |
| Intelligent call routing | Yes | Yes | |
| Natural conversation | Yes (82/100 score) | Yes | Click2Dash provides a score |
| Tow truck dispatch | Not confirmed | Not confirmed | **Critical — ask in demo** |
| Custom AI script | Not confirmed | Not confirmed | **Ask in demo** |
| Emergency routing | Not confirmed | Not confirmed | |

**Coverage Assessment**: Both offer AI agents as core features. Similar capability on paper. Click2Dash provides a self-reported "intelligence score" of 82/100 which at least shows they're measuring quality.

**Commentary**: The AI agent is where the rubber meets the road for an auto repair shop. Key things to test during demo:

- **Tow truck dispatch**: "It's 8 PM, my car broke down on I-95, I need a tow." Can the AI handle this? Can it trigger a dispatch workflow or at minimum capture the details and send an urgent SMS to the on-call person?
- **Service-specific handling**: "How much is a timing belt replacement on a 2019 Honda Civic?" Can the AI provide a range or does it just say "I'll have someone call you back"?
- **Spanish language**: If your market has Spanish-speaking customers, test this explicitly. Ask each vendor about multilingual support.
- **Handoff to human**: What happens when the AI can't handle something? Does it transfer live, take a message, or just hang up?

Request a live call simulation with each vendor using your actual scenarios.

## Decision Framework

### Choose Click2Dash If:

- Mobile app access is important for on-the-go management
- You want **InsightsCast audio coaching** (daily podcast-style performance recaps while driving to the shop)
- Marketing attribution and full-funnel ROI tracking is a priority (Use Case #2)
- You prefer a broader feature set with more polish
- You like the idea of an "intelligence score" for the AI agent (measurable quality baseline)

### Choose Olelo AI If:

- Fast deployment (1 week) is critical to getting started quickly
- You prioritize **conversion rate improvement** with specific, measurable results (15-20% proven)
- The **"manager rescue" feature** appeals to you — real-time alerts when a call is going sideways so a manager can intervene
- You want the simplest path from purchase to value
- You value specific result claims ($12K/month revenue uptick, 5 additional deals/advisor)

### Choose Neither (Build Custom) If:

- Outbound sales call scoring is non-negotiable (Use Case #3)
- You need deep integration with shop management software that neither vendor supports
- You want full control over AI scoring rubrics and playbook criteria
- You need both inbound and outbound in a single platform
- Long-term cost of ownership matters more than speed to deploy

## Recommendation

**Short-term (next 30 days)**: Demo both vendors using the checklist above. The outbound scoring question will likely be the deciding factor. Whichever vendor can address Use Case #3 — even partially — gets the edge.

**If forced to choose today**: Slight edge to **Click2Dash** based on broader feature coverage (mobile apps, InsightsCast, marketing attribution), but **Olelo** wins on specificity of results and speed to deploy. It's close enough that the demo experience should be the tiebreaker.

**If neither satisfies outbound scoring**: Evaluate stacking Rilla or a similar outbound-focused tool on top of your chosen vendor. Budget impact: likely $200-300/month additional, bringing total to $600-700/month for comprehensive coverage. Alternatively, this is the core value proposition of a custom-built Call Intelligence Platform — full inbound + outbound scoring in one system with complete control over playbooks and rubrics.
